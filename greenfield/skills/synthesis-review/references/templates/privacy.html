3. Lawful Basis & Consent
lawfulBasis:    {{privacy.lawfulBasis}}
consentManager: {{privacy.consentManager}}
GDPR Article 6 requires a documented lawful basis for each processing activity. Consent is only one of six bases — legitimate interest, contract performance, legal obligation, vital interests, and public task are alternatives that do not require a cookie banner. The consent manager (OneTrust, Cookiebot, custom) determines the technical mechanism for capturing and storing consent records.

5. DSAR & Data Export
dsar.sladays:  {{privacy.dsar.sladays}}
dsar.format:   {{privacy.dsar.format}}
dsar.workflow: {{privacy.dsar.workflow}}
GDPR Article 15 (right of access) and CCPA Section 1798.100 both grant data subjects the right to request a copy of their personal data. GDPR mandates a 30-day response SLA (extendable to 90 with notice); CCPA mandates 45 days. The DSAR workflow must enumerate all data stores that hold PII (from the PII inventory in Section 2) and produce a machine-readable export (JSON or CSV) covering all of them. If the PII inventory changes, the DSAR workflow must be updated to match.

Data residency is determined upstream by architecturalFraming.deploymentShape and the infrastructure region chosen during scaffolding. If GDPR is in scope, personal data transferred outside the EEA requires a legal transfer mechanism (Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decision, or Binding Corporate Rules). Pseudonymization (replacing direct identifiers with tokens) is a GDPR Article 25 data-protection-by-design requirement that reduces breach severity.
